ABOUT CLIENT

Amrock is a subsidiary of Rocket Companies that manages the title services and closing of the mortgage process.

OVERVIEW

Nova AI is an internal, AI-assisted workspace designed at Rocket Close to support exception-based, judgment-heavy workflows in regulated mortgage operations. The system helps subject-matter experts resolve complex decisions—such as identity matching, scheduling constraints, title exceptions, deed ordering, and document classification—without relying on manual document review or system hopping across Nova Workspaces, Atlas, Salesforce, or external vendor tools. This project explores human-in-the-loop AI and early agentic system design, with a deliberate focus on: Bounded autonomy, transparent reasoning, uncertainty communication, and preserving professional judgment in high-stakes environments.

MY ROLE

Lead UX Designer — 0→1 Conceptualization through Execution

Nova AI began as a 0→1 concept, initially co-created with the Director of UX and another senior designer. I now serve as the lead UX designer, owning the vision, strategy, and ongoing execution.

I was responsible for:

  • Defining the product and UX strategy
  • Establishing human–AI interaction principles
  • Research, synthesis, and workflow decomposition
  • Information architecture and interaction design
  • Conversation and prompt design
  • Prototyping, validation, and executive storytelling

In the early stages, there was no PM or PO. I partnered directly with UX leadership to define the product direction, then independently drove strategy until teams were ready to pilot execution. To accelerate alignment, we built a high-fidelity proof of concept using Figma, Figma Make, and ChatGPT—allowing us to demonstrate the system's behavior, not just its interface, and quickly secure executive buy-in.

Problem

Across Rocket Close, expert team members were spending significant time searching, verifying, and cross-referencing information before they could make a decision.

Many workflows required:

  1. Navigating multiple internal systems
  2. Manually scanning long, unstructured documents
  3. Matching ambiguous records across sources
  4. Re-entering information across tools
  5. Relying on training or memory to resolve edge cases

This pattern increased cognitive load, slowed throughput, and made work difficult to scale—especially in scenarios involving identity ambiguity and judgment-based risk.

Rather than asking: "How do we help users read documents faster?"

We challenged ourselves to ask:

What if users didn't have to read documents at all?

DESIGN CHALLENGE

How might we design an AI system that:

  • Performs the exhaustive investigation before a task reaches the human
  • Surfaces only decision-relevant information
  • Makes its reasoning legible
  • Communicates uncertainty and risk
  • And still allows humans to inspect raw evidence when needed?

Research & Insight

Through shadowing, interviews, workshops, and journey mapping, a consistent pattern emerged:

Team members were acting as human parsers of unstructured data.

Example: Identity Judgment in Large Documents

In a common scenario, a team member must identify the correct individual from a judgment or public record document containing hundreds of repeated names—for example, a document referencing "John Smith" dozens or hundreds of times.

Today, this process looks like:

  • Manually scanning page by page
  • Comparing attributes like birthdate, SSN fragments, addresses, and middle initials
  • Holding partial matches in memory
  • Spending hours verifying identity before proceeding

This work is cognitively expensive, error-prone, and poorly suited to human pattern-matching at scale.

REFRAMING THE WORKFLOW

Instead of making the human do first-pass investigation, Nova AI reverses the flow:

1

The AI processes the document first

  • Scans the entire record
  • Identifies candidate matches
  • Cross-references known attributes
2

The AI narrows the space of uncertainty

e.g. "Three possible matches identified"

3

The AI presents extracted, decision-ready information

  • Key matching attributes
  • Confidence indicators
  • Explicit risks and missing data
  • A plain-language explanation of why it believes a given match is most likely
4

The human makes the final call

  • With the option to inspect the original document if desired
  • But without being forced to start there

This transforms the human's role from document scavenger to decision-maker.

SOLUTION OVERVIEW

Three-Panel Decision Workspace

Nova AI is structured as a three-panel decision workspace:

Left Panel

A queue of exception-based tasks and AI conversations

Center Panel

A conversational interface that summarizes the task, explains what has already been checked, and surfaces candidate outcomes and reasoning

Right Panel

Structured decision cards that appear only when needed, presenting extracted facts, risk flags and confidence, supporting evidence, and available actions

The system intentionally prioritizes information compression and reasoning transparency over raw data exposure.

TRUST & TRANSPARENCY

Building Trust Through Explanation

A central challenge was asking users to trust less surface-level information than they were accustomed to.

To address this, Nova AI:

  • Explains its reasoning in natural language
  • Explicitly calls out uncertainty and missing signals
  • Allows users to ask follow-up questions
  • Preserves access to original documents as a fallback—not the default

This design acknowledges a key truth: trust is built through explanation, not assertion.

Human–AI Boundaries

Nova AI distinguishes between:

  • Automation — deterministic system actions
  • AI assistance — summarization, synthesis, confidence scoring
  • Agentic behavior — front-loaded investigation and orchestration

Clear Boundaries

The AI may:

  • Aggregate and reconcile data across systems
  • Narrow options to plausible candidates
  • Recommend a likely outcome with supporting evidence

The AI does not:

  • Finalize decisions
  • Override human judgment
  • Act without transparency

Nova frames the decision space. Humans retain ownership.

ADAPTIVE EXPERIENCE

Expertise-Aware Design

Nova AI adapts to user experience level:

Novice users:

  • Receive richer explanations
  • Are guided through reasoning steps
  • Can escalate or inspect evidence easily
  • Occasionally encounter "training moments" where the AI validates understanding

Expert users:

  • See concise summaries
  • Move quickly with minimal friction
  • Pull in additional detail only when needed

This prevents over-instruction for experts while building confidence for newer team members.
